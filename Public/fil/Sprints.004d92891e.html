Mga Sprint

Ayusin ang iyong mga open source na kontribusyon sa mga sprint. Subaybayan ang progreso ng pull request, kumita ng mga bounty, at pamahalaan ang iyong workflow bilang developer. Isang magandang side hustle para sa mga developer na gustong kumita sa pamamagitan ng de-kalidad na code.

Sprint Management — Organize Time-Boxed Development Work

Sprints on PrTask bring agile project management methodology to the bounty-based development model. A sprint is a time-boxed period — typically one to four weeks — during which specific tasks are planned, executed, and delivered. Sprint management allows clients and team leads to organize related tasks into focused work periods with clear start dates, end dates, and goals. This structured approach helps teams maintain momentum, track progress against deadlines, and ensure that development work aligns with broader project milestones.

Creating a sprint is straightforward: give it a name, set a goal that describes what should be accomplished, and define the start and end dates. Sprints progress through three statuses — Planned, Active, and Completed — giving you clear visibility into where each sprint stands. You can start planned sprints when the team is ready, complete active sprints when the time box expires, or delete sprints that are no longer needed. Each sprint tracks the tasks assigned to it, providing a focused view of what needs to be done within the time period.

Sprint management on PrTask integrates with the broader task and board systems to provide a complete project management experience. Assign tasks from the task list to specific sprints to create a sprint backlog, track completion rates as developers submit pull requests, and review sprint outcomes to improve planning for future iterations. Whether you are running a small open source project with a handful of contributors or managing a large-scale development effort across multiple repositories, sprint management helps you maintain predictable delivery cadences and keep your development work organized and on schedule.
